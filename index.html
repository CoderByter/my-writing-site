i. who i’d be, if i could feel



i’d be the kind of person who walks into a room and makes it feel softer; 

not because i’m loud or flashy, but because i’m grounded. 


someone whose presence feels like a cup of warm tea after a long day. 

i’d be quiet in the chaos, but steady. deeply curious. always listening with my whole self. 

emotionally layered, funny in the weird way, and secretly romantic.


i think i’d want a medium-sized family, just enough for loud dinners and soft hugs, but not so many that i get lost in the noise. 

maybe 2 siblings, one older, one younger. 


i’d want a tight bond with my mother, 

someone who passed down wisdom gently, not forcefully. 

a dad who listens more than he lectures. 

siblings i can cry-laugh with. family traditions like making playlists together, cooking barefoot, and watching the rain from the porch.


my lover? someone with spark and softness — they don’t have to be the loudest or most successful, but they’re emotionally there. present. 

playful. silly with me, but fierce in how they love. gender? doesn’t matter to me. it’s about soul, not shell. 

but i’d want them to challenge me, to build with me, to be my peace. 

someone who sees the versions of me i haven’t become yet, and believes in them.


kids? maybe one or two; not to live through them, but to raise humans who are kind and self-aware. 

who feel safe being themselves. 

or maybe no kids, just a life full of mentorship and love poured into others.


i’d want a life close to nature: big windows, slow mornings, books with folded corners, a garden with messy tomatoes. 

a studio with music always playing and a life filled with deep talks, art, purpose-driven work, and community.


i’d value honesty, kindness, emotional intelligence, growth, and creativity. 

i wouldn’t care for power or perfection. just connection. meaning. laughter. rest.


features? maybe tan skin, soft curls, warm eyes — eyes that say “i see you.” 

someone who wears oversized sweaters and paint-stained jeans and speaks with their hands.


culture? mixed. a patchwork. maybe raised between places — a bit of east, a bit of west. 

someone who codes in the morning and cooks with cardamom and turmeric at night. 


someone who says “inshallah” and “let’s manifest” in the same breath.





THE END.













ii. to stay human, kind, and awake in this world:





  • people are carrying things you can't see.
     everyone has invisible battles — grief, stress, heartbreak, fear.
     a little patience or kindness can shift someone’s entire day.


  • war is real, even when it’s not near you.
     while you’re scrolling or sipping coffee, someone somewhere is surviving bombs, violence, or displacement.
     stay aware. care. don’t look away.



  • poverty doesn’t mean laziness.
     so many are born into systems designed to keep them struggling.
     never shame the poor. choose compassion over judgment.


  • access is not the same as ability.
     just because you can do something doesn’t mean everyone else can — not everyone has access to safety, education, love, or rest.


  • animals and nature feel too.
     the world is alive and feeling.
     don’t forget the earth under your feet, the birds above, the quiet pain of animals.
     respect all life.


  • power without empathy is dangerous.
     just because you can doesn’t mean you should.
     leadership, influence, and success mean nothing without emotional intelligence.


  • humility is strength.
     you don’t always have to know everything.
     be willing to learn, apologize, evolve.


  • community is survival.
     you weren’t built to do this life alone.
     lean on people. uplift them. build safe spaces where everyone belongs.


  • joy is resistance too.
     in a world full of pain, choosing joy, laughter, art, and love isn’t shallow — it’s brave.
     make space for beauty, even in the darkness.


  • being human is messy.
     you’ll make mistakes. you’ll hurt and heal and grow.
     forgive yourself. stay soft. don’t give up on your own humanity.





THE END.













iii. soulful lessons


  • presence (being in the moment)
  • holding space (deep listening without judgment)
  • embodied awareness (feeling life through your body, not just your mind)
  • trusting the process (surrendering control and having faith in life)
  • emotional alchemy (turning pain into wisdom)
  • wholeness (honoring all parts of yourself)
  • authentic connection (real, heart-based relationships)



THE END.














iv. who i’d be, if i lived like a well-fed soul


i think if i lived like a human who knew she was built from the inside out 

— from cells and soul and softness — 

my days would start with water. not just a sip, but a slow, intentional drink. like telling my body, “hey, i’m here. we’re doing this together.”


i’d feed myself not just to be full, but to be well. food would be more than fuel 

— it’d be love, protection, a quiet little promise to my future self. 


i’d reach for leafy greens and deep colors — the reds of berries, the greens of spinach, the oranges of sweet potatoes. 


i'd think about fiber, about gut health, about magnesium and the way it helps me sleep. 

i wouldn’t obsess, but i’d care. because caring is the opposite of punishment.


breakfast would matter. protein in the morning, maybe eggs, maybe oats, something warm. 

iron paired with vitamin C. omega-3s for the brain i’m always using. 

zinc for my skin and the tiny battles happening under the surface that i’ll never see. 


i’d lower salt, not because salt is evil, but because i know the difference between seasoning my food and swimming in it.


i’d want to raise a family that doesn’t see health as obsession, but as stewardship. 

like tending to a garden you live in. 


i’d want my future kids to crave apples and lentils and water. 

not because they’re “good,” but because they feel good. a

nd if they ask why we eat like this, i’ll say — because you deserve to feel energized. because your body is your first home, and we take care of our homes here.


and then there’s the rest of the day.

the movement. i don’t think i’d work out to be smaller — i’d move to be here longer. 

walks after meals. pilates that stretch my body like it’s unfolding. maybe lifting weights like i’m protecting the girl i used to be — the one who didn’t feel strong.


my routine wouldn’t be about being productive. 

it’d be about being awake. 


i’d want morning light in my eyes, something green on my plate, good music playing while i fold laundry. 

i’d build a life with more vegetables than screens, more stretching than scrolling. 

and yet, i’d leave room for softness. butter on toast. chocolate on rough days. balance.


spiritually? emotionally? 

i’d check in with myself the way you check in with someone you love. 

“did you drink enough today?” “how’s your heart?” “what are you holding that’s not yours?”


i think the best humans 

— the ones who live fully — 

don’t live to impress. they live to connect. 


with their bodies. with others. with their own rhythm. 

they eat well, rest well, forgive often. they don’t skip breakfast. they say thank you to the sky. 

they walk with intention and kiss slowly and make enough space in their lives for both kale and cake.


and i think… that’s who i’d be. a girl who eats like she loves herself. who lives like her life matters. and who makes that kind of living feel contagious.



THE END.














v. two souls, unnamed

but deeply known



he doesn’t just love her.
 he studies her.
 like an ancient language,
 like something the stars buried on purpose.

he doesn’t just see her.
 he remembers her —
 from a life neither of them can name
 but both of them feel in their bones.


and her?

she gives him something he didn’t think he deserved:
 a home inside another human being.
 a place to land.
 to burn.
 to rebuild.


this isn’t soft love.
 this is full-body, leave-a-mark-on-your-soul love.
 the kind where you taste their absence hours before they leave.
 the kind where hands don’t just touch skin —
 they memorize it.


they don’t flirt.
 they devour.

eyes across a room like gravity.
 laughs that come too loud,
 and kisses that don’t ask permission.


he makes her laugh until she cries.
 until she gasps for breath and begs him to stop.
 but also:
 he ruins her composure with one sentence,
 one whisper in the dark,
 one “i dreamed about you again.”


they can go from sacred to sinful in the same five minutes.
 they speak in code and eye contact.
 they can read entire chapters in each other's silence.


no one touches her like he does.
 no one holds his attention like she does.

they’ve both tried to explain it.
 they’ve both failed.

because some love
 is not meant to be translated —
 only felt.


he watches her like art.
 but not the kind you hang in galleries.
 the kind you steal,
 hide,
 protect with your life.
 the kind you keep for yourself because the world doesn’t deserve to see something that pure.


and she?
 she isn’t gentle with him because she’s soft.
 she’s gentle because she’s powerful.
 and she knows exactly what it means to be tender with something rare.


they talk like lovers.
 fight like siblings.
 kiss like it’s the last time every time.

they have their own orbit.
 people feel it when they walk into rooms —
 a magnetic pull,
 a storm wrapped in honey,
 a bond you don’t dare interrupt.



this isn’t just love.
 this is alchemy.
 two people turning every past hurt into hunger.
 every scar into poetry.
 every night into religion.


and maybe it won’t be easy.
 maybe they’ll burn through rooms and wear out sheets and laugh at all the wrong moments.

but it will be real.
 and raw.
 and utterly unforgettable.


they are the kind of lovers
 you don’t get over.
 you carry them.
 even in silence.
 even in new cities.
 even if your hands never meet again.


but if they’re lucky —
 and god, maybe they are —
 they’ll grow old still laughing like teenagers.
 still sneaking kisses.
 still ruining each other’s plans
 with a single glance across the room.


because love like this doesn’t fade.
 it deepens.


the end.
 (but in their case, it never really ends.)
